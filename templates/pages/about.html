EDUCATION

Electoral District #3 has two educational districts, Sarbo school and Webbo school districts. Sarbo school district has two Junior high schools. Chea cheapoo Jr high school and Tom Wah Jr High school and seven elementary schools making a total of 9 schools in the district. Webbo school district has a total number of 22 schools both ECE, PRIMARY, JUNIOR HIGH and a senior high school. The district is a host of one of the three RTTI (rural teacher training institutes) of Liberia, Webbo rural teacher training institute and is greatly impacting our district.

HEALTH

The district has two health districts Sarbo and Webbo, Sarbo has one Health center with three clinics. Webbo health district has four clinics. The district is a host of three officials’ borders entries and seven unofficial entries making the district very porous along the cavalla river which forms the boundary with the Ivory coast. The livelihood of the people is predominantly agriculture particularly cocoa as cash crops. The district has huge potential of agriculture productivity in the near future.

Agriculture

The agriculture activities in District is characterized by rubber, oil palm, cocoa, rice, cassava and other mixed cropping activities, such as vegetables, roots and tubers, livestocks, animals rearing, fishing, corn, etc. However, farmers are unable to recover their investment due to little or no extention services, inadequate and inappropriate tools, equippment and technologies, lack of access to finance, lack of access to market, deplorable and/or lack of access to farms-to-market roads. All of these farming activities are carried out by individual household farmers. Hence organizing these farmers into category such as smallholder farmer groups (i.e. cooperatives, unions, companies, etc.) will increase food production, improve knowledge sharing, maximize the use of scared resources, reduce cost of investment and boost economic activities.

Information, Communication & Information (ICT)

There are about 78 towns with only seven (7) of the 78 town have access to telecommunications but with a very poor ICT penetration. The statistics show that only 8.9% of the 78 towns have access to ICT. There are three (3) orange towels, and one Lonestar towel with two radio stations.

Security

The District has two (2) Police stations (River Gbeh and Webbo Konowroken) with two major security check points in Gbeh and Konowroken.
